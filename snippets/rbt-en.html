Ringback tone service App

Tools

Photoshop, InVision, After Effects

 

Roles/Activities

Ideation, User Flow, UX/UI, Prototyping

América Móvil telecom needed a new way for users to access its ringback tone service.

 

My objective as the project product designer was to help them think of a new way for those users to activate and subscribe without having to use an IVR.

Understanding

The first step was to understand the company IVR (Interactive voice response) service to see how we could create a new way of giving access to this product without asking the user to call an IVR to subscribe and use the platform.

 

Analysis from the BI inside team and user research showed us that most of the users of this product were already using smartphones and had difficulties using the IVR system to subscribe and activate songs for their phone number accounts.

 

After some meetings with the commercial team from Brazil and Mexico, we came with the idea of creating an app which would connect to the service and make any activations and process needed, without the need to connect to any other service already in place.

 

Solution Design

The designing process started with the first app screens and after internal approval, I could finish the rest of the screens. Working with the dev team was another challenge as it was the first time I was doing an android app from scratch with them. Sticking with the google design documentation for android helped a lot, so many interactions and layout features were easier to implement.

As the app was to be used by the telecom clients, I could design a login process with the user's phone number.

The song activation process should be fast and easy. When clicking on the song item from the list, the activation buttons comes over for rapid activation.

If the user is using the carrier data, we can intercept it's phone account, and even see if he already has subscribed before. It was my first time thinking of different login and subscription alternatives using carriers and telecom data.

With this app, new functionalities could be implemented, like making groups for different people and an easier way to activate and deactivate songs.

Outcomes

The reception was the best possible. After the first release, I observed metrics from user access through Google Play Store and Google Analytics from inside the app.

 

Because of the success of this project, I could design the South American version of the app for Claro. This version would be available from Brazil to the rest of the Latam territories.

 

For the Brazilian version of the service, I helped the marketing team with branding, videos, ads, and even an intro video tutorial explaining the use of the service for new users.

 

Along with the app, I got tasked with helping a team develop a web version that would have the same features as the app.

 

The Brazilian version alone has more than 100k downloads.

 

The app helped make this service reach new heights, making it one of the most rentable services for the carrier in Brazil and other Latam countries.
