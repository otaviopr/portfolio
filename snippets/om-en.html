O&M Operations
And Maintenance

 

Tools

Sketch, Useberry, paper sketches

 

Roles/Activities

Ideation, User Flow, UX/UI, Prototyping and user testing

Engie is an energy sector company that had the necessity of creating a new O&M platform for operations and maintenance, as the product they were utilizing wasn't meeting their special needs.

 

My objective was to understand with the team inside the company their real needs, working with these and other stakeholders to recreate all the user flow, proposing and testing a new interface, and helping developers building the product.

Understanding

To understand the project and the real need from the stakeholders, we put together a Design Thinking meeting that was perfect to get to know some of the people involved and create a foundation for the whole project. All stakeholders were involved in the process, coordinators, senior managers, technicians, product owners, project managers, and I helped to facilitate it.

Making a Design Thinking was fundamental to understand the needs, ideas, and especially the pain points of the project, while at the same time putting everyone together to reach its main goals.

Some of the personas we created by the end of the Design Thinking process.

Collecting data

At the end of the two-day Design Thinking process we could gather a lot of information and beyond seeing the real scope of the project, which was really big, I could understand the workflow of the company and could, with the help of everyone involved, create a task and user flow.

One of the inicial task flow created at the Design Thinking

The user flow was really important not only to start imagining how the product would interact, and how the functionalities would appear but to help to see the scope of the project and assist the project manager to understand how he could plan the different parts of the product. For me, as the designer, it would help in the development of the pages, its interactions, and functionalities.

Solution Design

One important step was to start sketching all screen flows from the beginning, using the user flow as a foundation.

 

After that, I could start sketching some screens, thinking of how I would display features throughout the product.

 

As one of the main objectives was to have critical pieces of information displayed to the user, I designed a dashboard view, like a BI page, so coordinators, managers, and other high stakeholders could see real-time data that could be important to fast decision making.

 

The app version of the product was needed for maintenance personnel and on-site technicians to view and act on their service orders. An initial page with a calendar, tasks to be done, and a fast way to start a SO were fundamental for those users.

 

Besides that, a version for the clients was designed too, giving them the possibility to create SOs. After some interviews with the teams, I observed that many clients had problems utilizing past services and creating SOs, so we designed a way to create tasks where the client or even the technician could scan a QR code and the app would complete the form with the more technical part.

Equipment would have to get its QR codes registered at the platform too, so we develop a system that could turn IDs, new and pre-existing ones, into QR codes automatically.

In the main dashboard, we made various graphics to show the user interesting insights on their clients. Medium SLA deliveries, SO per equipment, and many other graphics to help the user understand how this client is performing, and how we can help him be better.

For the SO dashboard screen, the cards are color-coded for the different types of orders we have inside the system. The operational team had difficulties finding the next order to focus on SLAs, so dividing them in a list per day helped the team focus on the next task.

This dashboard was very special. Every time a technician sees a safety problem while executing an order, they can suspend a SO for protection reasons. Before this project, the safety team had a hard time tracking all SOs that was closed cause of protection reasons. Because of that, many SOs were getting lost, and some times even some technicians were risking their well being cause they couldn't have a safe way to suspend or ask the safety team for help resolving a SO. With this dashboard, we have an easy and fast way to see what SOs are having problems, a ranking of the equipment and client that have the most problems, so they can track if this is a safety problem for the tech team and alert the client.

The SO detail screen needed to have as much information possible about that task. Any problem and change of status can have commentary from the user explaining the status change. When changing the status of a protection problem, the user is obliged to describe what he is seeing and what is making him not able to complete that order. Doing this makes it easier for the safety team to track what problem is and get in touch with the technician for better understanding.

Testing

It was important to test and see how users would utilize the platform and its functionalities.

 

In the testing phase, I asked testers to do some actions inside the product, so I could validate the designed usability and could have feedback of how users were reacting, using insights from heatmap clicking, time to reach a certain page, user flow, etc.

 

 

Outcomes

As a result, we could see how much this product could positively impact the users from inside the company, and more importantly, integrate various teams that used to work in separate platforms.

 

This project will impact more than 1k users inside the company, and because of the good initial reception, the directors are already envisioning selling this product for clients that already use the company's services.
