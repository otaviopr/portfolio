While working for Engie, I got tasked at creating new functionalities for energy efficiency and asset management for its clients.

 

Understanding

One of the branches of the company were needing to create a new solution for asset management for its clients as a way to make them better energy efficiency.

 

My task was to work with the POs and stakeholders of this project to understand how we could make an easy way to manage and at the same time show metrics for energy efficiency for installed assets.

 

Solution Design

Many of the calculations and graphics that the technicians were using were created inside spreadsheets and didn't have any intelligence behind it.

 

The idea of the project was to install sensors inside all the assets the company has inside its clients, so my idea was to create a BI dashboard with many metrics from the sensors data.

After some user interviews, many technicians from the company said they needed more information about the site manager and real-time data from weather, as many assets were chillers and air conditioners that could be controlled more efficiently if you knew the temperature on site.

 

One of my main challenges was to understand those metrics, the calculations, and the ideas behind them.

 

I worked closely with the energy engineers doing many small brainstorm challenges to get their ideas and translate them to an easy to use interface.

 

My biggest question for them was always how this information can help the site technicians understand and act based on those metrics?

 

I enlisted some developers in some of the talks too, to make them understand and see how we could extract and use those calculations on the project.

The asset management dashboard needed to show all assets and an easy way to remotely send commands to turn on or off their power, including programming events for those assets.

 

At this dashboard, we have a calendar so the user can see all the assets that were already programmed, and an asset map. The map is very important to show where exactly this asset is located.

 

Another challenge was how we would show all information from the assets and its sensors in an easy way.

 

The user interviews showed that one of the main pain points from technicians was being able to see all technical metrics and info in one single page.

 

One way to approach was giving all assets its card with main information from the sensors, and when clicking on a historic icon, the user would be presented with a graphic with historical metrics and more detailed data that the sensor could send.

1/3

Outcomes

This project is still on development and is already being beta-tested in two customers.

 

The technical team already reported that with this platform they can better analyze real-time data, and those two clients will be able to reduce their energy cost for the next year.

 

The MVP version was already approved by the director's board, and the feedback was very positive. Future functionalities are being developed with the engineering team to make this project give even more energy efficiency metrics and data, like CO2 emissions.
