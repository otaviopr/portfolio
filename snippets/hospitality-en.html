Hospitality Guest Registration Form

Tools

Figma, Figjam, Maze, paper sketches

 

Roles/Activities

Ideation, User Flow, UX/UI, Prototyping and user testing

Zoox Smart Data is a startup specializing in data intelligence solutions. Their Hospitality product, which had been without updates for over five years, needed a major overhaul due to numerous client requests for new features and an improved user experience. Another key goal for this project was to reduce the time users spent completing the form.

As the Lead UX Designer on this project, my primary objective was to identify and address the gaps in the current product, propose innovative ideas for both UI and UX, and collaborate closely with the Front-end team to deliver a completely redesigned product to the market.

Organizing the scope and steps

As this was one of the first projects inside the company, I helped the PO and Stakeholders in defining how we were going to approach this project and what steps we needed to do in order to define and measure sucess.

 

Using the double diamond process, we could better define the steps we needed to reach each goal during the development of the project.

Discovery

After interviewing a pool of clients, primarily hotel supervisors and managers, we gathered more information about their pain points and needs for the product. It was vital for me to interview these users, as they were the most frequent and heavy users of the platform.

Devoloping / Solution Design

For the solution design, it was crucial to focus on the main pain points, especially the mobile aspect.

Additionally, it was important to create a basic and customizable design system that clients could easily edit to incorporate their visual identity.

By creating simple yet easily customizable design system components, we gave users more control over how the visual elements of the product align with their brand.

A start screen was created to explain the main goal of the product and provide tips to help users streamline the process and make it faster and easier.

Additionally, a secondary button offers information about the "FNRH" and explains why the hotel needs information from their users.

On the main screen, users can view all guests registered for hotel reservations. It was essential to implement a notification system for underage children on a reservation, specifically to inform users about the laws and requirements related to guests traveling with children.

At the top of the screen, users can always access their reservation details. Many users may have multiple reservations, and this feature provides a quick way to view relevant reservation information.

For the upload screen flow, we decided to create a "quick guide" screen to explain and provide tips on how to take better photos of user documents.

Devoloping / Testing

Using Maze, I was able to share the Figma prototype with multiple users for screen testing. The main goal of the test was not only to evaluate the interface but also to assess the new flows introduced in this version of the product.

I divided the prototype test in Maze into four steps, each representing one of the main flows of the product.

The results were impressive. Not only did we confirm that the new UX was effective, but we also gained clearer insights into some of the new flows introduced in this version.

On a scale from Very Bad to Very Good, we received a score of 4.7 out of a total of 35 responses.

When analyzing the screen flows for each of the four segments, we discovered that the "payment" segment was overly cluttered and difficult for users to understand.

As a result of the testing phase, I presented the analysis to the stakeholders and the Product Owner (PO) of the project, and we decided to make changes to the payment screen flow to simplify it.

Delivery

Creating thorough documentation for the development team is always a crucial part of my process.

For this project, the documentation I created in Figma not only assisted the front-end developers in understanding the defined business rules but also aided the Product Owner (PO) in creating the user stories for the developers.

Additionally, many front-end rules and definitions were included in the Figma file for easy reference by the development team.

Outcomes

We achieved outstanding results for this project. Several major clients who used our product expressed their satisfaction, particularly because one of the main goals was to reduce the time users spend completing forms. Another positive outcome was the enhanced customization options.

We implemented a rating modal at the end of each process to allow users to provide feedback, and the response was very encouraging.

Additionally, when analyzing the Google Analytics data from the website, we observed that many clients experienced a 25% increase in form completion rates compared to before.
